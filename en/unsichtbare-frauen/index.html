(III) What can I do?

You can't close data gaps alone, but every contribution counts.

  • Make care and housework visible and share it fairly. Take responsibility instead of just "helping".
  • Name the mental load: who keeps track of appointments, gifts, supplies? Share the planning, not just the doing.
  • Give girls and boys the same expectations, chores and compliments.
  • Mind your language: whoever is only implied but never named stays invisible.
  • Talk about salaries. Transparency is the first step against the pay gap.
  • In meetings, notice who gets interrupted and who gets credit for ideas.
  • Normalise parental leave and part-time work for all genders, not just mothers.
  • Check hiring and promotion criteria for unconscious bias.
  • Ask for sex-disaggregated data: in products, studies, statistics and your own organisation.
  • Question studies and tests: who was the sample? Were women tested?
  • Vote, and make equality an election issue. Reach out to local equality offices.
  • Support initiatives for better health and data research.
